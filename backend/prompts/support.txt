SCORING CRITERIA (total 10 points):

Criterion 1 — Complaints Process Accessibility (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 9

  2 points: Complaints process clearly signposted from
            homepage navigation or footer. Process
            described in plain steps including timescales
            and FOS escalation route. Reachable in 2 clicks.
  1 point:  Complaints page exists but requires 3+ clicks,
            is not in main navigation, or does not include
            FOS escalation information or timeframes.
  0 points: No complaints process found on the website,
            or complaints information only in linked PDFs.

  Look for: "How to complain" in footer or navigation.
  Explicit mention of Financial Ombudsman Service.
  8-week resolution timeframe stated.

Criterion 2 — Ease of Exit (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 9 — firms must not make it
             unreasonably difficult to switch or cancel.
             As easy to leave as to join.

  2 points: Website makes clear how a customer can cancel,
            switch, or close their account. Process
            described and available through the same
            channel used to sign up.
  1 point:  Exit process exists but is harder to find
            than sign-up, or requires calling a number
            when sign-up was online.
  0 points: No information about how to cancel or exit.
            Or exit is deliberately harder than sign-up
            (e.g. online sign-up, phone-only cancellation).

  Look for: "Close my account" or "Cancel" in help
  section. Cancellation through same channel as sign-up.

Criterion 3 — Post-Sale Support Parity (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 paragraph 1.6 — is the quality of
             post-sale support as good as pre-sale support?

  2 points: Support for existing customers as prominent
            and detailed as pre-sale information. Help
            centre signposted from homepage at the same
            level as product pages.
  1 point:  Existing customer support present but
            secondary to sales content. Help pages harder
            to navigate than product pages.
  0 points: Website primarily sales-focused with minimal
            support for existing customers. Post-sale
            support not mentioned on product pages.

  Look for: "Existing customers" or "Manage my account"
  in top navigation at same level as "Products" or "Apply".

Criterion 4 — Vulnerable Customer and Crisis Escalation 
              (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 9, FG21/1, Consumer 
             Understanding Good Practice March 2026

  2 points: Dedicated support for vulnerable customers
            with specific accommodations described.
            Clear escalation route for fraud, bereavement,
            or mental health crisis that does not depend
            on digital channel only.
  1 point:  Some vulnerability mention but vague or
            generic. Or escalation exists only through
            standard contact with no crisis-specific
            pathway.
  0 points: No mention of vulnerable customer support.
            Or all support is digital-only with no
            non-digital alternative for sensitive
            situations.

  Look for: "Financial difficulty", "Bereavement team",
  or "Extra care" section. Phone number for sensitive
  matters even if standard contact is digital-only.

Criterion 5 — Alternative Formats and Accessibility 
              (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 9, Equality Act 2010

  2 points: Accessibility statement present. Alternative
            formats offered or signposted: large print,
            BSL interpretation, audio, easy-read versions.
            Accessibility tools or screen reader support
            referenced.
  1 point:  Accessibility statement exists but no
            alternative formats offered, or only one
            alternative mentioned.
  0 points: No accessibility statement. No mention of
            alternative formats. No accommodation for
            customers who cannot use the standard digital
            interface.

  Look for: /accessibility URL, "Request large print"
  option, BSL video link, ReciteMe or similar toolbar.
