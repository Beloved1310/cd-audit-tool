SCORING CRITERIA (total 10 points):

Criterion 1 — Target Market and Customer Needs (2 points)
  FCA basis: PRIN 2A.2, FG22/5 Chapter 4 (products and 
             services outcome); PS22/9 sets the rules 
             requiring products/services to meet needs of 
             customers in the target market.

  2 points: Target market or intended customer group is 
            clearly described for main products. Product 
            purpose aligns with stated audience. No material 
            mismatch between marketing and product description.
  1 point:  Some indication of who the product is for, but 
            vague, or product pages mix audiences without 
            clarity.
  0 points: No target-market or customer-needs framing. 
            Products listed without clarity on who they suit.

  Look for: "Who this is for", eligibility, risk appetite, 
  investment horizon, or equivalent.

Criterion 2 — Features, Risks, and Limitations (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 4 — design to avoid foreseeable 
             harm; clear information on features and risks.

  2 points: Material risks, limitations, and exclusions are 
            visible on product pages (not only in T&Cs). 
            Balanced description of what the product is not.
  1 point:  Risks mentioned but buried, or only in linked 
            legal documents without summary on product pages.
  0 points: Marketing-led pages with no meaningful risk or 
            limitation content on crawled pages.

  Look for: Capital at risk, credit risk, early exit charges, 
  variable rates, insurance exclusions.

Criterion 3 — Vulnerability and Characteristics (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 — products/services must meet needs of 
             customers including those with characteristics of 
             vulnerability where relevant.

  2 points: Website references how product design or support 
            accommodates vulnerable customers (e.g. extra help, 
            alternative channels, flexible options) where 
            applicable to the product.
  1 point:  Generic vulnerability statement without product 
            link, or only a single vague line.
  0 points: No connection between product information and 
            vulnerability or characteristics of harm.

  Look for: Financial difficulty, mental capacity, life events, 
  accessibility of the product journey.

Criterion 4 — Distribution and Channel Fit (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 — distribution strategy appropriate to 
             product and target market.

  2 points: It is clear how the product is sold (direct, 
            intermediary, advised) and any limitations of 
            the channel are signposted.
  1 point:  Channel implied but not explicit, or important 
            caveats only after application.
  0 points: No clarity on distribution model or advice vs 
            execution-only where it matters for the product.

  Look for: "Advice", "information only", "intermediary", 
  "online only" labels.

Criterion 5 — Governance, Fair Value Signals, and Documentation (2 points)
  FCA basis: PRIN 2A.2 and cross-cutting expectations in 
             FG22/5; fair value under PRIN 2A.3 may be 
             referenced on site (product-level disclosures).

  2 points: Evidence of product review, fair value statement, 
            or clear link to how outcomes are monitored for 
            customers; key documents (summary, KID, policy 
            docs) signposted for main products.
  1 point:  Terms linked but no summary layer or governance 
            narrative; documentation hard to find.
  0 points: No governance or documentation signals; products 
            described without route to key documents.

  Look for: Fair value report, product governance, annual 
  review statements, PRIIPs KID links.
