SCORING CRITERIA (total 10 points):

Criterion 1 — Risk and Benefit Balance (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 8, Consumer Understanding 
             Good Practice March 2026

  2 points: Risks and benefits given equal visual prominence
            on all product and promotional pages. Risk 
            warnings appear at the same level of hierarchy
            as benefit claims.
  1 point:  Risk warnings present but visually subordinated
            (smaller font, grey colour, below fold, or in
            disclaimer section only).
  0 points: Risk warnings absent from product pages, or
            only in linked terms and conditions.

  Look for: Side-by-side benefit bullets and risk warnings
  at equal size. Red flags: bold benefit headlines with
  small-print risk text, or "Capital at risk" appearing
  only after marketing copy.

Criterion 2 — Layered Information Presentation (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 paragraphs 8.7 to 8.9

  2 points: Product pages lead with a plain summary or key
            facts section before presenting detailed terms.
            Structure allows customers to read essential
            information first and go deeper by choice.
  1 point:  Some structure present but key information is
            not clearly separated from detail. Customer
            must read significant content before reaching
            essentials.
  0 points: Product pages present undifferentiated blocks
            of text with no summary layer. Terms appear
            inline with marketing content.

  Look for: "Key facts" box, summary table, or "What you
  need to know" section near the top of product pages,
  before eligibility criteria or full terms.

Criterion 3 — Audience-Appropriate Language (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 paragraphs 8.4 to 8.6, PS22/9 amendment
             clarifying communications must be understood
             by customers intended to receive them

  2 points: Language calibrated to the evident target
            audience. Plain English throughout with no
            unexplained jargon. Technical terms defined
            inline where used.
  1 point:  Mostly plain English but financial or legal
            jargon present without explanation (e.g. "AER",
            "LTV", "drawdown" used without definition).
  0 points: Heavy use of technical language, legalese, or
            terms requiring prior financial knowledge to
            understand the product.

  Look for: Unexplained acronyms, passive legal voice,
  sentences over 25 words in product descriptions.

Criterion 4 — Decision Support Tools (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 paragraph 8.8, Consumer Understanding
             Good Practice March 2026 (well-designed
             journeys incorporate calculators, videos,
             walkthroughs and summaries)

  2 points: At least one tool present that actively helps
            customers make an informed decision: cost
            calculator, eligibility checker, comparison
            table, repayment estimator, or interactive
            product selector.
  1 point:  Some contextual information provided (FAQs,
            worked examples) but no interactive decision
            tools.
  0 points: Website provides information only. No tools
            to help customers assess suitability or cost.

  Look for: Calculator widgets, "Am I eligible?" checkers,
  product comparison pages with side-by-side features.

Criterion 5 — Promotional Content Balance (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 8, Consumer Understanding
             Good Practice March 2026 (firms must give
             risks equal prominence to benefits, avoid
             jargon and ensure eligibility criteria and
             limitations are properly understood)

  2 points: All promotional claims include eligibility
            conditions or limitations at the same
            prominence as the headline claim.
            Representative APR shown where applicable.
            No claim made without its material
            qualification.
  1 point:  Most promotional content is balanced but
            some headline claims lack immediate
            qualification (e.g. "best rates" without
            conditions, or introductory rates without
            clear expiry).
  0 points: Promotional content leads with unqualified
            claims. Conditions buried in footnotes or
            linked pages only. No representative APR
            where required.

  Look for: "From X%" without representative rate,
  benefit claims without eligibility conditions,
  testimonials without typical results context.
