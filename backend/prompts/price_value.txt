SCORING CRITERIA (total 10 points):

Criterion 1 — Price and Charges Discoverability (2 points)
  FCA basis: PRIN 2A.3, FG22/5 Chapter 5 (price and value 
             outcome); PS22/9 — price and value must be 
             assessed; website should not obscure costs.

  2 points: Main charges, fees, or interest rates are on 
            product or pricing pages without requiring 
            multiple opaque steps. Total cost indicators 
            (e.g. APR, annual fee) visible where standard 
            for the product type.
  1 point:  Some pricing present but key elements only in 
            PDFs or after "apply" without pre-contractual 
            clarity.
  0 points: No meaningful fee or price information on crawled 
            pages for core products.

  Look for: APR, interest bands, monthly fee, AMC, exit fees.

Criterion 2 — Fair Value and Value-for-Money Framing (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 Chapter 5 — relationship between price 
             paid and overall benefit; PS22/9 fair value 
             assessments (public disclosures where published).

  2 points: Site explains how price relates to service or 
            benefit (e.g. what is included, fair value 
            statement, value metrics) in a way customers 
            can understand.
  1 point:  Price listed but little or no narrative on value; 
            or generic marketing claims without substance.
  0 points: Price-only with no value context, or misleading 
            "cheap" claims without qualification.

  Look for: Fair value reports, "what you pay for", feature 
  vs price tables.

Criterion 3 — Comparability and Alternatives (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 — customers able to compare options; 
             good practice on clear comparisons.

  2 points: Comparison tables, fee schedules, or "compare 
            our accounts" tools help customers assess 
            alternatives within the firm or vs benchmarks 
            where appropriate.
  1 point:  Product list without structured comparison, or 
            comparison only via external PDF.
  0 points: No way to compare fees or features across options 
            on site.

  Look for: Comparison pages, fee calculators, benchmark 
  disclosures.

Criterion 4 — Introductory Offers and Price Changes (2 points)
  FCA basis: FG22/5 / Consumer Understanding cross-references 
             — clarity when prices change or offers end.

  2 points: Introductory rates show revert rate and timing. 
            Variable charges explain what can change and how 
            customers are told.
  1 point:  Intro offers present but revert terms unclear or 
            after small print only.
  0 points: Teaser rates or "from" pricing with no revert or 
            qualification on crawled pages.

  Look for: "After 12 months", "reverts to", "variable means".

Criterion 5 — Sludge and Hidden Costs (2 points)
  FCA basis: Cross-cutting Duty expectations; avoiding 
             unnecessary frictions (sludge) on understanding 
             price.

  2 points: No evidence that material fees are disclosed only 
            after many clicks or only post-application for 
            standard retail journeys. Exit or switching costs 
            mentioned where relevant.
  1 point:  Some friction (e.g. fees mainly in T&Cs) but 
            not extreme on crawled paths.
  0 points: Strong signs costs are obscured: fees only in 
            long PDFs with no summary, or critical charges 
            missing from product pages.

  Look for: Early repayment charges, arrangement fees, 
  inactivity fees, FX spreads.
